Dog Days

“Almost half of all Australians aged 16 to 85 years — 7.3 million people — will experience mental illness at some point in their life.” Dog Days is an app that helps people, especially animal lovers, to ease the stress and provide virtual furry companions to improve their mental wellbeing.

Introduction

This is the first group project in my digital product design journey. We have been randomly assigned as three in a team and work as UX/UI designers together.
As students, we started forming ideas by throwing some favourite apps up and discussing how we used them daily. We found common ground within the three of us in stress and our ways to manage it.
Modern life is tough, and while some of us may currently have some rituals that help us step away from a busy day of work or study or life... whether that’s a knock-off drink or a relaxing commute - a lot of people don’t, especially those that have had changed routines due to working from home.
It turns out that our team each had a method of destressing, and it was always about watching animals do their thing. That's how this product came about.
